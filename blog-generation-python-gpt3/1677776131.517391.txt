  <h1>The Genius of Leonardo da Vinci</h1>
  <p>Leonardo da Vinci was a man of many talents. He was a painter, sculptor, architect, musician, scientist, mathematician, inventor, engineer, and anatomist. He lived during the Italian Renaissance, a period of great cultural and intellectual activity. His works are some of the most admired in the world.</p>
  <p>Leonardo's paintings are among the most famous in the world. The Mona Lisa is perhaps his most famous and admired painting. Other masterpieces include The Last Supper and Virgin of the Rocks. His paintings are known for their use of light and shadow, as well as his use of perspective and depth.</p>
  <p>Leonardo was also an accomplished sculptor. He created a number of sculptures, including the bronze statue of David and the horse statue of Milan. He was also an architect, designing a number of churches and other buildings. His designs included the dome of St. Peter's Basilica.</p>
  <p>Leonardo was also a scientist and inventor. He studied anatomy and made detailed drawings of the human body. He also studied mathematics, optics, hydraulics, and flight. He made detailed drawings of machines and inventions, such as a flying machine and a tank. Many of his inventions were ahead of their time and were not built until centuries later.</p>
  <p>Leonardo da Vinci was a true genius, one of the greatest minds of all time. His works of art, inventions, and scientific discoveries continue to inspire and amaze us today.</p>
